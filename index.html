Phi Theta Kappa

Phi Theta Kappa is the official honor society for two-year colleges, as recognized by the American Association of Community Colleges.

Find Out More

Welcome aboard!

Phi Theta Kappa provides its members the opportunity to improve their communication, leadership, teambuilding, and goal-setting skills through hands-on leadership activities and online video courseware. Members can design a self-paced plan allowing them to choose how competitive they want to be, from one star to five stars. The requirements at each level provide members with experience based on Phi Theta Kappaâ€™s four Hallmarks: Scholarship, Leadership, Service, and Fellowship. Members can also build an online portfolio that will showcase their accomplishments, and that they can share with scholarship providers, admissions officials, and potential employers.

Membership Benifits

Transfer Scholarships

Members may apply for more than $37 million in transfer scholarships provided exclusively to Society members by more than 700 senior institutions.

Letters of Recommendation

Members can request letters of recommendation to their college admission counselors, scholarship officials or potential employers.

Membership Credentials

Members receive The Golden Key Membership Pin, Membership Certificate, and Membership ID Card.

Volunteer Opportunity

Members have ample opportunity for individual growth and development through honors, leadership and service work.

These were the list of questions asked of the two clinicians of Newport Academy on November 16th in an interview conducted with the members of PTK. Dante Clemente an officer of PTK conducted the interview for the College Project. Q&A followed.

  • How do you define therapeutic recreation?
  • How have patients at this facility benefitted from therapeutic recreation?
  • What are the main obstacles and limitations when utilizing therapeutic recreation?
  • Is there a different goal with the practice of therapeutic recreation compared to conventional therapies?
  • Are there any patients who are initially opposed to receiving this therapy? What are their expectations when receiving it?
  • Is there any difference in effectiveness based on demographic, such as age, gender, or ethnicity?
